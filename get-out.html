Get Out (2017)

Rating: 9.5/10

Genre: Horror


Get Out is a groundbreaking horror movie that tackles issues of race and identity in a way that is both thought-provoking and terrifying. Directed by Jordan Peele, the film tells the story of a young African-American man named Chris (played by Daniel Kaluuya) who visits the family of his white girlfriend (Allison Williams) for a weekend getaway. From the moment Chris arrives at the family's isolated estate, things begin to feel off-kilter. The family's well-meaning but awkward attempts to relate to Chris become increasingly uncomfortable, and he begins to uncover a sinister plot that threatens his very existence. One of the film's strengths is its ability to blend horror and social commentary seamlessly. Peele uses horror tropes to explore the insidious nature of racism, and the way that it can manifest in subtle and unexpected ways. The film also subverts expectations in surprising ways, taking familiar horror movie elements and turning them on their head. The performances in the film are outstanding, with Kaluuya delivering a nuanced and powerful portrayal of a man caught in an impossible situation. Williams also gives a standout performance as Chris's girlfriend, whose motivations are constantly called into question. "Get Out" is a timely and important film that speaks to the current political and social climate. It is a testament to the power of horror as a genre, and its ability to provoke thought and inspire discussion. If you are a fan of horror movies that push boundaries and challenge your preconceptions, then "Get Out" is a must-see.
