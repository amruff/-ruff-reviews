The Girl on The Train (2018)

Rating: 9.9/10

Genre: Psychological Thriller


"The Girl on the Train" is a 2016 mystery thriller directed by Tate Taylor and based on the novel of the same name by Paula Hawkins. The movie stars Emily Blunt as Rachel, a divorced woman who becomes entangled in a missing persons case after witnessing something from her train window. The movie is a gripping and suspenseful thriller that keeps you guessing until the very end. The pacing is expertly handled, with the tension building steadily as the mystery unfolds. The movie's twists and turns are well-executed, and the payoff is satisfying. Emily Blunt delivers a standout performance as Rachel, bringing depth and nuance to her character. Her portrayal of a woman grappling with addiction and trauma is raw and compelling, and she anchors the movie with her emotional range. The supporting cast is also strong, with standout performances from Haley Bennett and Rebecca Ferguson. The movie's cinematography is also noteworthy, with the use of close-ups and blurry shots effectively conveying Rachel's sense of disorientation and confusion. The setting of suburban New York is also well-utilized, with the dark and moody visuals adding to the overall atmosphere of the movie. Overall, "The Girl on the Train" is a well-crafted and engaging thriller that will keep you on the edge of your seat. It's a movie that is elevated by its strong performances, particularly Emily Blunt's, and its expert handling of the source material. If you're a fan of the mystery thriller genre, this is definitely a movie worth watching.
